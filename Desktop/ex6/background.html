She had a crew of 300 and carried well over 2,000 passengers, mostly in Third Class, and could carry more than 12,000 tons of dry bulk and refrigerated cargo. Entering service in 1903, she provided steady and reliable service from Liverpool to Boston in the summer and from New York to Mediterranean ports in the winter, transporting mostly Hungarian emigrants.
